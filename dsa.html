Data structures and Algorithms

A DSA course taught by ex-IITian and employee in one of the top product based companies

Description

Hello guys, I am Kartavya Kothari, a computer science student at IIT Bombay pursuing my masters degree. I also bagged a placement offer from one of the top product based companies, Dream11. It took a lot of hard work to reach where I am, and what surprises me is that learning new things wasn't the hard part. The hard part was to find and compile resources to learn core concepts. Let me tell you about two basic dreams you will have.

[Dream 1] How to get a job in the CS industry

  • Learn a programming language
  • Learn web development, app development
  • You’ll now be a good coder
[Dream 2] How to get the best and the highest paying CS jobs
  • Learn a programming language
  • Learn data structures, learn algorithms
  • Do competitive programming
  • You’ll now be a good programmer
90% CS engineers will have the first dream, along with tons of people who aren’t even CS engineers, because it is EASY.

If like me, you want to go for the second dream then here are some of the problems that you’ll face too!
  • Learning DSA as a college subject is the primary reason you’ll start moving towards the first dream because they WILL teach you how to score rather than how to get better.
  • One of the best books for DSA is “Introduction to Algorithms” which, if you have ever opened it, is not at all an “Introductory book”. (I own a copy) You’ll need to be consistent in prep like never before and doing it with college studies is tough to manage.
Using my experience with the above, I have prepared a data structure and algorithms learning strategy. In this training course I will be guiding you to analyze and implement common algorithms building your basics right from scratch. This course is for people who are ready to put in the work and believe in the second dream. If you follow the lecture with dedication and complete all my designed assignments, I guarantee you a boosted career path and a greater understanding of concepts than 50% of top 100 JEE rankers at the same age.

You will start by learning about the c and c++ basics, then jump into learning about data structures. From there, I will guide you about how to interpret and implement all difficult concepts. My tutorial also covers various different GATE level variations to simple data structures like arrays, stacks, queues, sorting etc. You will also learn about searching, hash tables, recursion, and backtracking algorithms. Finally, you will also have a familiarity with trees, balanced trees, decision trees, and network algorithms.

Once you have completed this computer based training course, you will be fully capable of analyzing and implementing algorithms, as well as be able to select the best algorithm for various situations and that is why you’ll be paid the big bucks.

The course will run for 2 months (1st month Data structures and 2nd month Algorithms). The cost to enroll is 10,000 rupees. Since I plan to personally mentor each student, it would be a really small batch and there may be an interview involved. I will only be selecting students who show a spark that they would like to be the top 1% in CS in their area.
